    Front page

    I am a computational physicist at the Centre for Nanoscience and Nanotechnology (C2N), situated in the heart of the Silicon Vauve on the Saclay plateau in the greater Paris area. The C2N is a joint research unit of the Centre National de la Recherche Scientifique (CNRS) and Université Paris-Saclay.

    As a member of the Novel Magnetic Devices group at the C2N, I lead the theoretical and computational research on nanomagnetism and spintronics. My activities are mainly focused on nonlinear and stochastic magnetization processes in nanostructured magnetic materials. I have held a tenured research scientist position at the CNRS since 2004.

    Since 2023, I am also an Associate Editor at npj Spintronics.
